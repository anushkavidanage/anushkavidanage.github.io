About

I am a Ph.D. candidate in the School of Computing at the Australian National University. Currently I am working in the area of privacy attacks on privacy-preserving record linkage (PPRL) techniques. PPRL is the process of linking records across databases from different sources, while at the same time, preserving the privacy of the entities whose personal identifying details are stored in those databases. My other research interests include data privacy, data mining, and cryptography.


I am also a passionate photographer studying photography as a hobby. I enjoy travelling and taking photographs of landscapes, cityscapes, woodlands, and wildlife.
